The hidden line item

The liability shield
you're actually paying for.

Workers' comp, GL, payroll taxes, ACA benefits, proper classification — all baked into the hourly. You're not buying labor. You're buying out of the liability chain.

01

Workers' Compensation

Every crew member is covered under our policy. If a stagehand sprains an ankle during load-in, the workers' comp claim runs through us — not your event-day GL policy and not your producer carrier. Your insurance stays clean.

02

General Liability

$1M / $2M general liability coverage on every shift. Certificate of insurance (COI) issued to your venue or production company on request — usually within one business day, no upcharge.

03

Payroll Taxes

FICA, FUTA, SUTA — all employer-side burden carried by us, included in the hourly. The 7.65% employer FICA that contractors quietly shift to producers? It's already in our rate.

04

ACA Compliance

We're an ACA-applicable large employer. Our W-4 staff receive ACA-compliant minimum-essential coverage offerings. You inherit zero healthcare-mandate exposure from booking us.

05

Worker Classification

Every staffer is W-4. We don't run 1099. Florida and Texas have aggressive misclassification penalties — and the federal DOL is increasingly pushing audits onto producers who use contractor crew. With SMPL, your classification posture is unambiguous.

06

Certifications

Lead riggers carry ETCP or SPRAT. Lift / forklift operators are OSHA-certified. Tech-leads carry vendor-specific certs (DiGiCo, MA, BlackTrax, etc.) where the venue requires. Training records on request.

The math producers miss.

When you book a 1099 crew company at $40/hr instead of SMPL at $48/hr, the $8/hr "savings" usually evaporates the first time you back-of-envelope the true cost:

  • Workers' comp insurance gap — for non-W-2 labor, your event-day policy carries the workers' comp exposure. Add-on premium runs $4–$6/hr per crew member on a typical event policy.
  • Misclassification exposure — DOL audit penalties on a misclassified contractor average $5,000–$25,000 per worker. One bad audit on a 20-crew show pays for SMPL premium over 10 years of bookings.
  • "Admin fees" — 1099 shops typically add 8–12% admin on top of the contractor rate. SMPL has zero admin percentage. Bake-in pricing.
  • Insurance certificates — many contractor shops charge $50–$150 per COI issued. We don't.

On a 100-hour show, the SMPL all-in usually lands $50–$100 above a 1099 quote — for an order of magnitude less liability exposure.
