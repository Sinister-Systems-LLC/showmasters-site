The bet

Why we built
the crew that shows up.

We started in 2002 with a simple idea: producers needed a crewing partner that knew the difference between a stagehand and a technician, and stopped blurring the two on the invoice. Twenty-plus years and 1,700+ clients later, that's still the bet.

Today we run from two offices — Orlando (Hub) and Dallas — with eight operational hubs across the country, 130+ crew on payroll, and a traveling team that has staffed events in 33 states. Every staffer is a W-4 employee. Every shift has a Crew Lead. Every invoice arrives within 72 hours of load-out. No hidden fees. No admin percentages.

Three divisions. One number. The crew you booked is the crew that shows up.

Three divisions

One company,
three lanes.

01

Show Masters Production

Live event stagehands, riggers, technicians, lift operators, show management. The original division, founded 2002. Where the company started.

02

AllStar Exhibits I&D

Trade show installation & dismantle, exhibit AV, experiential builds. The division that handles convention-floor work alongside the union show-services contractor.

03

SMPL Systems

Audio, video, lighting, and control-systems integration for clients who want one phone number for both labor and gear. Specialty division for hybrid productions.

Two decades on the dock

A few of the
turning points.

  1. 2002
    Year one

    Founded in Orlando

    Show Masters Production opens with a small bench of W-4 stagehands and one core promise: stagehands billed as stagehands, technicians billed as technicians, never blurred on the invoice.

  2. 2008
    Trade-show launch

    AllStar Exhibits I&D division launches

    Dedicated I&D crew for trade shows at Orange County Convention Center, OCC, and Gaylord Palms. The convention bench grows to 30 hands across the year.

  3. 2014
    Dallas hub opens

    Texas operations begin

    The second office opens in Dallas to service the rapidly-growing North Texas event market. Stadium concert and corporate plenary calls drive the expansion.

  4. 2018
    SMPL Systems launches

    Gear + crew under one number

    Audio, lighting, video, and control-systems integration as a third division. Producers can book labor and gear from a single point of contact for hybrid productions.

  5. 2022
    Post-pandemic rebuild

    Hub network grows to eight

    Hubs added in California, Nevada, Colorado, Tennessee, Georgia, and Louisiana. Traveling-crew model formalized for shows outside the home markets.

  6. 2026
    Today

    1,700+ shows. 33 states. 130+ crew.

    The bench is the deepest it has ever been. The 72-hour invoice still ships every time. And the Crew Lead who ran your last call is still here for the next one.

Standards

What we actually
stand for.

Attention to detail

The small things on a 14-hour load-in are what make the show feel polished. We notice them, every time.

Communication

You hear from us before there's a problem, not after. The Crew Lead carries the radio for a reason.

Follow-through

The crew you booked is the crew that shows up. Same names on the call sheet, same hands on the dock.

Employee welfare

Our crew likes working here. That's why they're still here on year fifteen. Continuity is the whole point.
