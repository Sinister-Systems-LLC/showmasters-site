CASE 01 CORPORATE PLENARY

ORANGE COUNTY CONVENTION CENTER · ORLANDO, FL · 2026

Fortune-100 sales kickoff: 4 days, 38 crew, zero change orders.

38crew on rotation
4days onsite
1,420attendees
$0in change orders

The brief

A Fortune-100 tech company brought their annual sales kickoff to OCCC West Hall. Main plenary in a 1,500-seat ballroom + six concurrent breakout rooms + a sponsor expo floor. Hybrid streaming to 800 remote sales reps. Two video walls (54-panel + 36-panel), four IMAG screens, full LED-wash lighting rig, six audio zones.

The crew shape

Day 1 load-in: 12 stagehands + 6 riggers + 2 lift operators + 2 Crew Leads. Truss in the air by 18:00. Day 2 setup: same hands plus 4 audio techs + 4 lighting techs + 4 video techs + 1 Stage Manager + 1 Technical Director. Days 3–4 show days: full team at 38 on rotating shifts. Day 4 strike: 14 hands + 4 riggers + 2 lift ops, room emptied by 02:30.

What went right

The Tech Assessment we ran during the brief stage identified a video-tech shortage two weeks out — the producer had under-counted because they'd treated IMAG and video-wall ops as one role. We pre-emptively added two techs, the producer signed off, and the show ran without a single "we need another body" moment. The TD did the show-call cleanly; the Stage Manager kept the run of show tight; the riggers finished early, freeing them to help the AV cable runs.

The number that mattered

Zero change orders. The estimate was the invoice. The producer's finance team had budgeted a 15% labor contingency. They came in at 0% over the estimate.

What the producer said

"I've run this event for six years. This is the first year I haven't had to write a change-order memo to finance. The estimate held."

VP of Corporate Events
CASE 02 ARENA TOUR STOP

AMWAY CENTER · ORLANDO, FL · 2026

Headliner pop tour: 52 crew, 8-hour overnight, no incidents.

52crew on call
14:00load-in start
06:30strike complete
0safety incidents

The brief

A platinum-touring pop artist with a 6-truck show pulled into Amway Center for a single-night arena date. Flying truss with 220+ motorized fixtures. Two video pods on automation. A B-stage runway extending 40 feet into the bowl. Pyro cues — separate licensed crew. Doors at 19:30, show at 20:00, hard load-out start at 22:30 right after the encore.

The crew shape

4 up-riggers (ETCP-certified) + 8 ground-riggers spent the first 4 hours putting motors and truss in the air. 20 stagehands handled gear cases, cable runs, AV setup, B-stage assembly. 4 audio techs, 4 lighting techs (programming on grandMA3), 4 video techs handled systems. 3 Crew Leads — one per zone (FOH / Stage / Strike Coordination). 1 Show Caller paired with the touring TD.

What went right

The tour's traveling rig manager had worked with our crew on a previous date — he flagged us early as the local labor he wanted. That continuity saved 90 minutes of "where does this go" on load-in. The arena's grid presented an unusual point that the touring designer had marked as a maybe; our up-rigger lead caught it on the walk-through, ran the math, and approved the point pre-load. No mid-load surprises.

The number that mattered

Zero safety incidents on a 52-crew, 16-hour push. The riggers came down clean, the strike crew finished by 06:30, the trucks rolled at 07:00. The tour manager listed SMPL as preferred-vendor for their next Orlando date 11 months later.

What the producer said

"Touring shows live and die on the local crew. This one lived. I'll book them again."

Tour Production Manager
CASE 03 TRADE SHOW I&D

ORANGE COUNTY CONVENTION CENTER · ORLANDO, FL · 2026

Healthcare conference: 120 booths, 4-day install, 2-day dismantle.

120exhibitor booths
6total I&D days
28crew on rotating shifts
72hto detailed invoice

The brief

A medical-device association's annual conference brought 120 exhibitor booths to OCCC's North Hall. Booths ranged from 10×10 standard pop-ups to 50×50 island custom builds. Six pre-rigged AV-equipped sponsor booths needed dedicated technician support. Strict OCCC dock schedule — drayage slots assigned by booth ID, no flexibility on arrival windows.

The crew shape

This was handled through SMPL's AllStar Exhibits I&D division — purpose-built for trade-show install/dismantle. 22 I&D crew + 4 forklift operators + 2 boom-lift operators + 2 Crew Leads + 1 Personnel Coordinator working dock check-in. Crew rotated on 10-hour shifts across the 4-day install.

What went right

The Personnel Coordinator pre-mapped every exhibitor's dock slot, drayage arrival, and crew assignment 72 hours before the show floor opened. When 18 of the 120 booths had drayage arrive on the wrong day, we had crew float on standby and re-routed without missing anyone's slot. The producer's conference team did zero firefighting on the floor.

The number that mattered

The detailed invoice landed in the producer's inbox at 14:18 on the second day after load-out — well inside the 72-hour SMPL guarantee. Line-by-line: 120 booths × itemized hours × crew rate. No "approximate" lines, no "TBD" entries. Their finance team approved it the same week.

What the producer said

"The dock-slot Tetris is what kills these conferences. SMPL solved that before we even noticed it was a problem."

Director, Conference Operations
